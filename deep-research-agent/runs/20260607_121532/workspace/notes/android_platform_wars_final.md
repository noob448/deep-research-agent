# Android Rise & Platform Wars (2008-2015) — FINAL Research Notes

## 1. Android Founding & Google Acquisition
- Android Inc. founded Oct 2003, Palo Alto — Andy Rubin, Rich Miner, Nick Sears, Chris White
- Goal: open-source mobile platform
- Google acquired Aug 17, 2005 for ~$50M; Rubin became VP
- Sources: src_6225c0 (Wikipedia), src_a9f867, src_ea3529

## 2. HTC Dream / T-Mobile G1 (2008)
- First Android phone, Oct 2008, T-Mobile
- 3.2" touch screen, 3.15MP camera, slide-out QWERTY keyboard
- Design influenced by Danger Sidekick (Rubin's prior company)
- Sources: src_dace51 (Mint Mobile), src_8a678a (WIRED)

## 3. Android Version History (2008-2015)
- Cupcake (1.5, Apr 2009) → Donut (1.6) → Eclair (2.0/2.1) → Froyo (2.2) → Gingerbread (2.3) → Honeycomb (3.0, tablet) → Ice Cream Sandwich (4.0, 2011) → Jelly Bean (4.1-4.3) → KitKat (4.4) → Lollipop (5.0, 2014)
- ICS unified tablet/phone UI; Lollipop introduced Material Design
- Source: src_8ece30 (Android Wiki)

## 4. Samsung Galaxy S Series
- Galaxy S launched 2010 — Samsung's first flagship Android
- Became most popular Android line globally through Galaxy S6 (2015)
- Samsung controlled largest share of Android OEM ecosystem
- Sources: src_679164, src_5d396d

## 5. iOS vs Android Market Share
- KB internal: Android 0→22.7% in 2 yrs, hit 70% by end 2012
- Gartner Apr 2011: Android 38%+, iOS peak ~19.4% (src_ce3fd7)
- IDC 2010: iOS share projected to decline through 2014 (src_c3aa0e)
- The Register Dec 2013: Android 70.9% Europe, WP 10.2% (src_8a00c9)
- Statista chart available (src_6f537e)

## 6. Google Play vs App Store
- Google Play beat App Store in total downloads (~45% more c.2014)
- App Store generated more revenue (higher per-user spending)
- Google Play became largest app marketplace by volume
- Sources: src_ca2dd6, src_684662, src_87692d

## 7. Windows Phone Failure
- Replaced Windows Mobile + Zune; Metro UI with Live Tiles
- "App Gap": chicken-and-egg — too few users → developers skip → too few apps → users skip
- Marketplace.org (2012): "there just aren't as many apps" (src_ce620e)
- SpriteGenix: "greatest strategic error in Microsoft history" — acknowledged by Gates, Ballmer, Nadella (src_10e475)
- PopSCI: app gap "most significant factor" (src_d97e82)
- WP brief spike: 10.2% Europe Q3 2013; couldn't sustain
- Source: src_b3b45e (Wikipedia)

## 8. BlackBerry 10 Decline
- BB10 delayed until 2013 — "disastrous quarterly earnings report" (src_f4fe50, TechRadar)
- Lacked native app clients — ecosystem too thin (src_9a5179)
- Q3 2014: Revenue $793M (down from $1.1B year prior) — NYT (src_25ed96)
- By 2015, BB released Android phones (Priv), abandoning BB10
- BBC: "increasingly impossible battle as Apple and Samsung" dominated (src_f1b287)
- Sources: src_383667 (Wikipedia), src_958ef4

## 9. Nokia + Microsoft
- "Burning Platform" memo: CEO Stephen Elop (Feb 9, 2011)
- Nokia abandoned Symbian (73% share in 2006 per KB), adopted Windows Phone
- Microsoft bought Nokia devices for $7.2B; wrote down $7.6B (KB internal)
- Slate: "crazy idea" that backfired (src_0389c8)
- Sources: src_a81b68, src_4db023, src_4fdd73

## 10. Android Fragmentation & OEM
- Charlie Kindel (2012): "very real and very problematic for end users, developers, mobile operators, device manufacturers, and Google" (src_e36129)
- Open source = many OEMs, many screen sizes, many OS versions on market simultaneously
- Gartner: 99.6% of new smartphones run Android or iOS (src_bf4ef6)
- OEM ecosystem: Samsung dominated; HTC, LG, Motorola, Sony all competed
- Fragmentation didn't stop Android's growth but created developer challenges
