# Android Rise & Platform Wars (2008-2015) — Research Notes

## 1. Android Founding & Google Acquisition
- Android Inc. founded Oct 2003 in Palo Alto by Andy Rubin, Rich Miner, Nick Sears, Chris White
- Goal: open-source mobile platform
- Google acquired Aug 17, 2005 for ~$50M — Rubin became VP at Google
- Sources: src_a9f867, src_6225c0, src_ea3529

## 2. HTC Dream / T-Mobile G1 (2008)
- First commercial Android phone, launched Sept/Oct 2008
- 3.2" touch screen, 3.15MP camera, slide-out QWERTY keyboard
- Design influenced by Danger Sidekick (Rubin's prior company)

## 5. iOS vs Android Market Share
- KB: Android went 0→22.7% in 2 years, hit 70% by end 2012
- Gartner 2011: Android 38%+, iOS peaked ~19.4%
- IDC 2010 forecast: iOS share would decline through 2014
- Statista chart available: smartphone OS market share breakdown
- The Register (Dec 2013): Android 70.9% Europe, Windows Phone 10.2%
- Sources: src_ce3fd7, src_c3aa0e, src_6f537e, src_8a00c9

## 6. Google Play vs App Store
- Google Play surpassed App Store in total downloads (45% more ~2014)
- App Store generated more revenue despite fewer downloads
- Google Play became largest app marketplace by volume
- Sources: src_ca2dd6, src_684662, src_75587a, src_87692d

- BB10 meant to replace aging BB OS, compete with Android/iOS
- By 2015, BlackBerry released Android phones (Priv)

## 9. Nokia + Microsoft
- "Burning Platform" memo by CEO Stephen Elop (Feb 2011)
- Nokia abandoned Symbian, adopted Windows Phone
- Symbian had been dominant (73% in 2006 per KB)
- Microsoft acquired Nokia devices for $7.2B, later wrote down $7.6B (KB)
