# Pre-iPhone Smartphone Research Notes

## IBM Simon (1992-1994)
- Brainchild of IBM engineer Frank Canova
- Prototype "Sweetspot" showcased at Comdex 1992
- IBM + BellSouth partnered to commercialize; Motorola declined manufacturing
- Launched August 1994 as "IBM Simon Personal Communicator"
- PDA with cellular connectivity — not called "smartphone" at the time
- Features: phone calls, fax, email, pages, touchscreen, apps (calendar, address book, calculator, notepad, sketch pad)
- Price: ~$899 with contract; sold ~50,000 units
- Source: Mobile Phone Museum (src_0a6f13), Android Authority (src_4b1a0e)

## Nokia Communicator Series (1996+)
- Nokia 9000 Communicator (1996): first QWERTY keyboard in a mobile handset
- 8MB memory, 33MHz processor, clamshell design
- 9210 Communicator (2001): one of first Symbian OS devices, color display, full QWERTY, multitasking
- The term "Nokia Communicator" commanded respect roughly 2000-2012
- Sources: Economic Times, Medium article (src_5f5a31), ResearchGate (src_2878d4)

## Ericsson R380 (2000)
- First mobile phone commercially branded as "smartphone"
- The term "smartphone" was first used by Ericsson for its GS 88 (Penelope) prototype
- Used EPOC Release 5 (predecessor to Symbian OS)
- Flip-open design revealing touchscreen
- Sources: NewAtlas (src_053476), Mobile Phone Museum (src_0a6f13)

## Windows Mobile / Pocket PC
- Based on Windows CE
- Pocket PC announced at CES 2000 by Bill Gates
- Stylus-driven, scaled-down Windows with Start Menu
- Mobile Office included
- Later rebranded to Windows Mobile
- Source: MobileRead Wiki (src_3ef01d), Wikipedia Pocket PC (src_a15a3c)

## Symbian OS
- Originated from Psion's EPOC32 (1994, 32-bit C++)
- Symbian Ltd. founded 1998 by Psion + Nokia + Ericsson + Motorola
- Dominance: >65% market share by 2007 (KB source says 73% by 2006)
- Nokia's Series 60 (S60) UI toolkit (AVKON) — keyboard-focused
- Later shifted to Qt for touchscreen support
- Nokia's 2011 shift to Windows Phone marked the end
- Sources: Hotbot (src_cd35d4), Symbian Foundation (src_a85951)

## Definition Evolution
- Early "smartphone" = PDA + cellular phone convergence
- Key features: OS, web browsing, app capability, email
- Term "smartphone" coined by Ericsson ~1997/2000
- Before Ericsson, devices like IBM Simon were called "PDA with communication capabilities"
- Sources: TechTarget (src_036e3c), Mobile Phone Museum (src_0a6f13)
