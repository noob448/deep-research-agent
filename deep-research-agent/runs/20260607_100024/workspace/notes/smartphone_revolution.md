# Smartphone Revolution 2007-2013 - Research Notes

## KB Findings
- 2007 iPhone: multi-touch revolution, finger-direct interaction, App Store two-sided platform
- Android: open source + OHA alliance, rapid catch-up
- 2012 Q1: Samsung 29.1% share, Samsung-Apple duopoly formed
- China "big four": Huawei, Xiaomi, OPPO, vivo

## App Store (2008)
- Launched July 2008 with ~500 apps (src_d3383a)
- First time solo devs could reach tens of millions users globally
- Revolutionized software distribution - from blocking developers to owning distribution (src_79b0e6)

## iPhone 2007 Launch
- Announced by Steve Jobs on January 9, 2007 at Macworld 2007 (src_0fbfa7)
- First mobile phone to use multi-touch technology
- Launched later in 2007 (June 29, 2007 in US)
- Full transcript available: european-rhetoric.com (src_dd5d99)

## Android Birth
- Google acquired Android Inc. in 2005 for ~$50 million (src_08fc7d)
- Open Handset Alliance announced November 2007 - included HTC, Sony, Samsung, Sprint, T-Mobile, Qualcomm
- First Android phone: HTC Dream / G1, released October 2008 (src_10ec99)
- Android version 1.0, upgradable to 1.6

## iOS Evolution (Key Models)
- iPhone 3G (2008): 3G connectivity, App Store
- iPhone 3GS (2009): Faster, video recording
- iPhone 4 (2010): Retina Display introduced (src_db5d97), new glass/steel design
- iPhone 4S (2011): Siri voice assistant introduced
- iPhone 5 (2012): Larger 4-inch screen, Lightning connector
- iPhone 5S (2013): Touch ID fingerprint sensor introduced (src_c35982)

## Samsung Galaxy S Series Rise
- Galaxy S3: >40 million units sold globally (src_4d3db3), heavy marketing vs Apple
- Galaxy S4: 10 million sales in 27 days (vs 50 days for S3) (src_b7f2f0)
- Galaxy S3 impacted iPhone sales in UK market (src_1f68cc)

## Market Share Data (OS)
- BlackBerry: 9.6% (2007) → 16.6% (2008) → 19.9% (2009), then decline (src_f00a92, Gartner data)
- Late 2012: Android claimed ~70% of world smartphone share (src_28037d, Strategy Analytics)
- BlackBerry, Symbian, Windows Phone combined: sank from 25.1% (late 2011) to 7.9% (late 2012) (src_28037d)
- 2009 landscape: fragmented across Symbian, BlackBerry, Windows Mobile, early iOS and Android (src_6e5f00)

## Nokia/Symbian Decline & Microsoft Acquisition
- 2011: Nokia abandoned declining Symbian, embraced Windows Phone exclusively (src_222f31)
- 2010: Nokia announced Symbian 3 as last-ditch effort to compete (src_6ae8ba)
- September 2013: Microsoft acquired Nokia's smartphone business for $7.2 billion (src_222f31)
- RIM/BlackBerry losing share in North America to iPhone and Android since ~2010-2011 (src_2aabf2)

## Global Smartphone Shipments & Market Data
- 2010 OS market share (Gartner): Symbian 37.6%, Android 22.7%, RIM 16%, Apple iOS 15.7%, Microsoft 4.2% (src_69b9bb)
- Q1 2012: 152.7 million units shipped worldwide
- Q1 2013: 216.2 million units shipped (41.6% YoY growth), smartphones became 51.6% of total phone shipments (src_a668eb)
- 2012 full year: ~725 million smartphones shipped, 42% of mobile phone market (src_49a2d3)
- 2013: smartphones reached 55% of mobile phone market (src_49a2d3)
- Late 2012: Android ~70% of smartphone market (Strategy Analytics) (src_28037d)

## China Market
- Chinese smartphone vendors: 15% of global shipments (~2011) → 21% (~2012) → 25% (Q3 2013) (src_a98c8a)
- Huawei climbed to No.2 in China with 16% share (2014 period)

## HTC
- Wikipedia page (src_f96149): Founded 1997, began as ODM/OEM; became a leading Android manufacturer in early Android era

## Motorola Droid
- Released late 2009, Motorola's "comeback" product (src_d466d5)
- First Android phone with serious US carrier backing (Verizon partnership) (src_6630f5)
- Launched with Android 2.0 Eclair - multiple account support, improved Bluetooth, enhanced camera (src_3d42df)

## Academic Sources
- Teece (2018): "Profiting from innovation in the digital economy" - covers wireless standards and ecosystems (src_8c5e24, DOI: 10.1016/j.respol.2017.01.015)
- Jacobides et al. (2018): "Towards a theory of ecosystems" - modularity enabling ecosystem emergence, relevant to smartphone platforms (src_530a40, DOI: 10.1002/smj.2904)

## Nokia-Microsoft Deal Details
- Stephen Elop (Microsoft exec) became Nokia CEO in 2010
- Feb 2011: "Burning Platform" memo - acknowledged Symbian crisis (src_d6f85c)
- 2011: Nokia abandoned Symbian, partnered exclusively with Microsoft for Windows Phone (src_222f31)
- Sep 2013: Microsoft acquired Nokia's phone business for $7.2 billion (src_fce46e)
- Microsoft wrote down $7.6 billion just two years later (src_fce46e)

## iPhone Sales Data
- Original iPhone (1st gen): Total 6,124,000 units sold, discontinued July 15, 2008 (src_7b8f15)
- Cumulative through Q2 2008: ~5.4 million (src_9e2c81)
- Statista reports dramatic unit sales increase 2007-2024 but exact annual figures behind paywall (src_d44a04)
