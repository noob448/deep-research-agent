# iPhone Revolution & Android Rise (2007-2012) - Research Notes

## iPhone 2007 Launch
- Announced: Jan 9, 2007 at Macworld, San Francisco (Steve Jobs keynote)
- Released: June 29, 2007
- "Three revolutionary products": widescreen iPod with touch controls + revolutionary mobile phone + breakthrough internet communicator → all in one device
- Slogan: "Today, Apple is going to reinvent the phone"
- Key innovation: Multi-touch capacitive touchscreen (no stylus), full web browser, visual voicemail
- Competition at time: Moto Q, BlackBerry, Palm Treo
- Initial limitations: 2G only, exclusive to Cingular/AT&T, no 3rd-party apps, $499/$599 price
- Source: src_228193, src_71ef44, src_d6daca

## App Store (2008)
- Launched July 10, 2008 alongside iPhone 3G
- Created the modern mobile app ecosystem
- Transformed iPhone from a device into a platform

## Android 1.0 & HTC Dream/G1
- Android beta: Nov 5, 2007
- Open Handset Alliance formed
- Android 1.0 released: Sept 23, 2008
- First Android phone: HTC Dream / T-Mobile G1 (2008)
- Key features: slide-out QWERTY keyboard, touchscreen, Android Market, Google integration
- Source: src_ba115f (Wikipedia, not fetched)

## Samsung Galaxy S
- First Galaxy S launched 2010
- Galaxy S II (2011) was a massive hit
- By Q1 2012, Samsung shipped 42.2M smartphones (29.1% global smartphone market)

## Market Share / Sales Data
- Q3 2007: Nokia 63% of smartphone market, total market only 17M handsets/quarter
- 2011: Global smartphone shipments hit 491.4M (up 61% YoY)
- Q3 2011: Windows Phone 1.5%, BlackBerry 11.0%, Samsung Bada 2.2%
- Q1 2012: Samsung 29.1%, Apple 24.2% of smartphone market
- Q1 2012: 144.9M smartphones shipped globally (42.5% YoY growth)
- Samsung overtook Nokia for total mobile phone market leadership (first since 2004)
- Nokia China: 29.9% (2011) → 3.7% (2012)
- Source: src_9e7b11, src_def659, src_22f0b6 (search results)

## Nokia / BlackBerry / Windows Phone Decline
- Nokia: Dominated pre-iPhone (63% Q3 2007), failed to adapt, partnered with Microsoft (2011)
- Windows Phone: Q3 2011 only 1.5% share, down from 2.7% in Q3 2010
- BlackBerry: 11% Q3 2011; still 33% UK share Dec 2011 but declining fast
- Microsoft eventually purchased Nokia mobile division (£4.6bn, announced 2013)
